FAQs About Ransomware

  • What is ransomware?

    Ransomware is a type of malware that holds a victim’s sensitive data or device hostage, threatening to keep it locked unless the victim pays a ransom to the attacker.

  • How can I protect my business?

    Implement strong cybersecurity measures, regularly update software, and train employees on security best practices. Implement important training on how to detect social engineering techniques such as phishing.

  • What should I do if I am attacked?

    Do not immediately pay the ransom. Contact cybersecurity professionals and law enforcement for assistance.

  • How does business size affect ransomware risk?

    Larger businesses may face higher demands due to perceived ability to pay, but all sizes are at risk.

Cybersecurity Tips:

  • Regularly back up your data.
  • Use strong, unique passwords.
  • Implement multi-factor authentication (MFA).
  • Train employees to recognize phishing emails.
  • Install antivirus software and keep it updated.
